## An Area I Care About: Blockchain for EV Charging

As electric vehicles become more widespread, the charging infrastructure behind them needs to handle complex, multi-party transactions — between EV owners, charging station operators, grid operators, and energy providers. Blockchain offers a promising foundation here: it enables trustless, transparent, and automated settlement of energy transactions without relying on a central authority. I am interested in how this decentralized model can make EV charging more interoperable, fair, and scalable across different networks and countries.

## Ways Generative AI Could Help in This Area

- **Smart contract drafting and auditing:** Generative AI can help developers quickly write and review smart contract code for charging session billing, reducing errors and accelerating deployment.
- **Explaining on-chain data to users:** AI can translate raw blockchain transaction records into plain-language summaries, helping non-technical EV drivers and station operators understand their energy usage and payment history.
- **Generating simulation scenarios:** AI can produce realistic test cases and load scenarios to stress-test blockchain-based charging networks before they go live, identifying edge cases in settlement logic or token economics.
